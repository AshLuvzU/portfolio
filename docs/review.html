The Things We Lost In The Flood

The Things We Lost In The Flood was made by Awkward Silence Games, developed by Dean Moynihan, and music by Leafcuts.

The premise of the games seems to be to just cross the water that has covered the earth while collecting bottles with messages, floppy disks, and new parts for your boat. The game starts you off on a remote island where the main character has to drag a boat full of holes to the water. From there you just keep paddling across the new ocean that has formed, occasionally there will be a bottle or a boat part that floats by. Towards the middle of the journey there is a door with a number on it, I didn't have enough of the disks to open it, but there is probably a secrete behind it.

The story behind this games seems to deal with some heavy topics such as grief and loss in general. The games has you write down topic such as "write something personal" and "describe someone you love", then tells you to throw the bottle into the ocean.
